PUBLICATION HISTORY

John Milton’s poem “On His Blindness” was likely composed around 1655
during his later years after he became completely blind.
However, the poem was not published as an individual work during his lifetime.
Instead, it was included posthumously in collections of his poetry.

Publication History

1. First Posthumous Publication (1673)

“On His Blindness” was first published as part of “Poems, etc.
upon Several Occasions”, a collection of Milton's sonnets and shorter works.

2. Later Editions

The poem was subsequently included in “The Works of John Milton”
(various editions compiled in the 18th and 19th centuries).
It was classified under Sonnet XIX, as Milton did not originally title the poem “On His Blindness.”
This title was later attributed by editors to reflect the central theme of the poem.

3. Modern Recognition

Over time, “On His Blindness” became one of Milton’s most anthologized and studied sonnets
celebrated for its universal themes of faith, patience, and divine service.
